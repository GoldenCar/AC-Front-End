Important Notice
Compliance with Notification Requirement
 
All licensed persons should note and acknowledge this important circular regarding the need to notify the Securities and Futures Commission ('SFC') of actual or potential breaches and credit-related events in addition to all other notifications.
These events are summarised below:
a. any material breach of any regulations, or where it suspects any such breach, infringement or non-compliance by:
i. itself;or
ii. staff appointed to conduct business with clients or other licensed or registered persons;
b. the winding-up, dissolution or bankruptcy or the making of any receiving order or arrangement or composition with creditors;
c. the bankruptcy of any of its directors;
d. where disciplinary measure against it are taken in connection with its business;
e. any material failure or error in its trading, accounting, clearing or settlement systems or equipment; and
f. any material breach, infringement or non-compliance of market misconduct provisions.
FURTHER GUIDANCE
The source of these requirements is the Code of Conduct, which states that reporting should take place upon the occurrence of an event. The SFC has interpreted this literally to mean that firms should make reports as soon as practicable and prior to taking remedial action, making investigations and getting legal advice.
However only breaches which are material need to be reported. Examples of what the SFC considers to be material are contained in the circular. These are:
• Suspected unlicensed dealing activities;
• Suspected unauthorised trading activities;
• Non-compliance with the suitability requirements under paragraph 5.2 of the Code of Conduct as supplemented by the corresponding frequently asked questions;
• Breaches of the Securities and Futures (Keeping of Records) Rules;
• Breaches of the Securities and Futures (Contract Notes, Statements of Account and Receipts) Rules; and
• Non-compliance with the order recording requirements under paragraph 3.9 of the Code of Conduct.
When a breach has occurred or suspected firms should therefore:
• Investigate and log the incident (notifying the relevant ROs and Compliance Officer);
• Assess whether the breach is material;
• Report to the SFC;
• Develop, implement and document remediation.
Firms should update their policies to ensure that such reporting can be made promptly and are not subject to further events (such as obtaining approval from headquarters)
Firms are advised to ensure that their compliance officers and managers-in-charge are notified as soon as possible whenever a breach has occurred or suspected. If a report is made, firms should be ready to respond to follow up questions such as what remedial actions will be taken.
